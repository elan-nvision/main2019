Social Cause

On 6th September 2018,the age long struggle of legalisation of homosexuality came to an end. The recent upliftment of the LGBT community can be seen as a sign of society finally accepting those who think and feel differently from the norm. In the past, even influential people such as Alan Turing who have come out as homosexuals, have had their images tarnished and were seen as freaks of nature. Homosexual intercourse was declared illegal and was considered a criminal offence under Section 377 in 1860 and remained the same even after India gained independence and right to equality came into effect. By the late 2000s, the LGBT situation in India started improving. In 2009, Section 377 was decriminalised by the Supreme Court but later overturned. Since 2014, transgenders have been able to register under the third gender. In 2016, transgenders were allowed to vote for the first time under the ‘others’ category. Same sex intercourse has been legalised but same sex marriage is still a criminal offence and is believed to become legal in the next few years. The government has also tried to directly uplift transgenders by giving them various privileges such as reservations for jobs and education. Although major inroads have been made, there's still a long a way to go before the LGBT community can truly feel accepted by our society.
